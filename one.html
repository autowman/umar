Qatar Cargo Global Logistics Process

A Worldwide Network from Origin to Destination

Qatar Cargo's Global Network

Frankfurt, Germany
1

Initial Cargo Collection

The journey begins with cargo collection from customers through various channels, ensuring proper documentation and initial inspection.

  • Online booking portal and phone reservations
  • Collection through freight forwarders and sales representatives
  • Initial inspection and security screening
  • Preparation of Air Waybill (AWB) and export documentation

Key Collection Hubs:

Frankfurt (Germany), London Heathrow (UK), Chicago O'Hare (USA), Shanghai Pudong (China), Singapore Changi, Mumbai (India), Johannesburg (South Africa), São Paulo (Brazil)

Shanghai, China
2

Warehouse Operations

After collection, shipments enter Qatar Cargo's state-of-the-art warehouses for processing, classification, and storage according to their specific requirements.

  • Classification by cargo type (general, perishable, pharmaceutical, live animals)
  • Temperature-controlled zones (2-8°C, 15-25°C, -20°C)
  • High-value storage with enhanced security measures
  • Real-time tracking using barcode/RFID technology

Major Warehouse Facilities:

Doha (Qatar) - Main 55,000 sqm facility at Hamad International Airport, Amsterdam Schiphol (Netherlands), Hong Kong International, Los Angeles (USA), Bangkok (Thailand)

Doha, Qatar
3

Flight Planning & Load Preparation

Careful planning ensures optimal use of aircraft capacity and proper handling of different cargo types during loading.

  • Aircraft payload capacity assessment and optimization
  • ULD (Unit Load Device) preparation and allocation
  • Weight and balance calculations for flight safety
  • Special handling requirements implementation

Primary Load Planning Centers:

Doha (Qatar) - Central operations hub, London (UK), Atlanta (USA), Brussels (Belgium) - Pharmaceutical excellence center, Hong Kong - Asian operations center

Hamad International Airport
4

Transportation

Qatar Cargo utilizes a modern fleet of aircraft to transport shipments globally while monitoring them throughout the journey.

  • Dedicated freighter aircraft (Boeing 777F, Boeing 747-8F)
  • Hub-and-spoke model centered on Doha's Hamad International Airport
  • Real-time shipment tracking via GPS/IoT devices
  • Temperature and condition monitoring for sensitive cargo

Main Air Routes:

Asia-Europe (Shanghai/Hong Kong/Singapore to Frankfurt/London/Paris), Trans-Pacific (Hong Kong/Shanghai to Los Angeles/Chicago), Middle East-Europe (Doha to Frankfurt/London/Brussels), Trans-Atlantic (USA to Europe)

New York, USA
5

Arrival Processing

When cargo arrives at the destination, it undergoes systematic unloading, documentation processing, and interim storage if needed.

  • Priority-based unloading sequence
  • Import documentation and customs clearance processing
  • Quality checks to ensure shipment integrity
  • Temporary storage allocation or cross-docking operations

Key Arrival Processing Centers:

New York JFK (USA), Chicago O'Hare (USA), Frankfurt (Germany), London Heathrow (UK), Milan Malpensa (Italy), Bangkok (Thailand), Sydney (Australia), Johannesburg (South Africa)

Amsterdam, Netherlands
6

Last-Mile Distribution

The final phase involves delivering cargo to recipients efficiently and reliably, with proper documentation and confirmation.

  • Route optimization for efficient delivery
  • Vehicle allocation based on shipment characteristics
  • Electronic proof of delivery documentation
  • Customer satisfaction verification

Distribution Networks:

Europe (integrated trucking network across EU countries), North America (specialized partners in USA and Canada), Asia (dedicated distribution centers in major Asian cities including Singapore, Hong Kong, Shanghai, Tokyo), Middle East (Qatar-based fleet serving GCC countries)
